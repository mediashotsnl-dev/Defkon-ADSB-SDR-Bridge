# Privacy Policy - ADS-B SDR Bridge

Last updated: July 23, 2026

## Overview

ADS-B SDR Bridge processes radio data from a compatible USB SDR receiver on the Android device. The app makes decoded aircraft data available only through local loopback connections on the same device for use by compatible applications.

## Data collection

The app does not collect, sell, share, or transmit personal data to the developer or to advertising or analytics providers. It does not require an account and does not contain advertising, analytics, tracking, or payment SDKs.

## Local processing and storage

The following information may be processed or stored locally on the device:

- USB receiver status and SDR configuration;
- locally received and decoded ADS-B aircraft broadcasts;
- app preferences and service state;
- limited diagnostic crash information generated by the app.

Decoded aircraft data is served only on Android loopback addresses (`127.0.0.1`) and is not uploaded by the Bridge to the developer's servers. App backups are disabled. Local app data can be removed by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling the app.

## Permissions

The app uses USB host access to communicate with compatible SDR hardware, notification permission to show foreground-service status, and foreground-service/network capabilities to operate the local Bridge while active. Internet permission also allows the user to open the public source-code and license pages and, when needed, the store page for a compatible SDR driver. The Bridge itself does not upload decoded ADS-B data to the developer.

## Third-party links and software

The app may open external pages hosted by Google Play or GitHub at the user's request. Those services apply their own privacy policies. The app includes open-source components whose licenses and corresponding source information are available inside the app and in the public source repository.

## Children's privacy

The app is a technical utility intended for drone and radio users. It is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.
